📊 Bank Marketing Campaign Analysis – Power BI Dashboard

This project analyzes a bank’s direct marketing campaign dataset to understand customer behavior and identify factors influencing term deposit subscription rates.

The dashboard was developed using Power BI to transform raw campaign data into actionable insights for marketing decision-making.

🎯 Project Objectives

Analyze customer characteristics affecting deposit subscriptions

Evaluate marketing campaign performance

Identify patterns in call duration, balance, and contact strategy

Visualize conversion trends across time and customer segments

📈 Dashboard Overview

The dashboard includes several interactive visualizations designed to explore campaign performance.

KPI Metrics

Total Contacts – total number of customers contacted

Total Subscriptions – number of successful deposit subscriptions

Conversion Rate Gauge – percentage of successful campaign outcomes

Time Series Analysis

Tracks how subscription conversions change across different months, helping identify seasonal marketing performance.

Channel Performance

Compares marketing effectiveness across different contact methods (e.g., cellular, telephone).

Campaign Funnel

Shows the relationship between number of campaign contacts and the likelihood of successful subscription.

Customer Segmentation

Interactive slicers allow filtering by:

Month

Job type

Age bucket

Contact channel

Housing loan status

Personal loan status

Campaign contact count

This enables exploration of which customer segments convert best.

Duration vs Balance Scatter Plot

This visualization analyzes the relationship between:

Call duration

Customer bank balance

Campaign contact frequency

The scatter plot reveals that longer call durations tend to correlate with higher subscription rates, indicating the importance of customer engagement during marketing calls.

🔎 Key Insights

Some insights from the analysis include:

Customers contacted via cellular channels show higher conversion rates.

Longer call duration is strongly associated with successful subscriptions.

Customers with higher balances are more likely to subscribe to deposits.

Certain job categories and age groups demonstrate stronger conversion patterns.
